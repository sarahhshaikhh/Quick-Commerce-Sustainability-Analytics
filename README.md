# 🌱 Quick Commerce Sustainability Analytics

### Business Intelligence, Machine Learning, and Game Theory for Sustainable Decision-Making in Quick-Commerce

> 📄 This project forms the basis of the research paper **"Business Intelligence and Strategic Equilibrium for Attaining Sustainability in Trending Quick-Commerce Platforms"**, accepted at the **International Conference on ICT for Sustainable Development (ICT4SD 2026).**

---

# 📌 Business Problem

Quick-commerce companies operate in a highly competitive environment where profitability, operational efficiency, and sustainability must be balanced simultaneously. Traditional reporting often evaluates financial, environmental, and social performance independently, making strategic warehouse planning and resource allocation difficult.

This project develops a Business Intelligence framework that integrates operational analytics, Machine Learning, and Game Theory to evaluate warehouse sustainability and support data-driven business decisions.

---

# 🎯 Project Objectives

The project aims to:

- Evaluate warehouse performance using operational data.
- Measure sustainability through **Economic, Environmental, and Social (ESG)** indicators.
- Predict warehouse profitability using Machine Learning.
- Segment warehouse performance using clustering techniques.
- Support strategic decision-making through Game Theory.

# 📊 Methodology

The project follows an end-to-end Business Intelligence workflow:

1. Data Collection
2. Data Cleaning & Preprocessing
3. KPI Engineering
4. Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
5. Interactive Power BI Dashboard Development
6. Machine Learning Modeling
7. Strategic Analysis using Nash Equilibrium
8. Business Insight Generation & Recommendations

---

# 🤖 Machine Learning

## Random Forest Regression

Used to predict warehouse profitability based on operational and sustainability metrics.

**Model Performance**

- **R² Score: 0.956**

---

## K-Means Clustering

Applied to segment warehouse hubs based on sustainability and operational performance, enabling comparison of high-performing and underperforming warehouse groups.

---

# 📈 Key Business Insights

The analysis enabled the following insights:

- Identified high-performing and underperforming warehouse hubs using sustainability metrics.
- Compared warehouse performance across Economic, Environmental, and Social dimensions.
- Evaluated operational efficiency through profitability, delivery performance, and resource utilization.
- Predicted warehouse profitability using Machine Learning.
- Segmented warehouses into similar sustainability groups to support strategic planning.

---

# 💼 Business Recommendations

Based on the analysis, organizations can:

- Prioritize investment in high-performing warehouse hubs.
- Improve operational efficiency in underperforming locations.
- Monitor sustainability performance through Business Intelligence dashboards.
- Balance profitability with environmental and social objectives during expansion planning.
- Support long-term decision-making using predictive analytics and strategic modeling.
